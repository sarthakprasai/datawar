Data used for business:

How it's used:

  • Predictive Execution: Companies use AI to move beyond "static reports" to "agentic workflows," where AI systems autonomously identify market shifts and execute tasks—like adjusting supply chains or launching targeted ad campaigns—without manual intervention.
  • Hyper-Personalization: Businesses leverage deep behavioral datasets to provide "vibe-based" digital experiences, where websites and services instantly reconfigure themselves for each user, significantly increasing conversion rates and customer loyalty.
  • Operational Intelligence: By integrating IoT and real-time analytics, industries like manufacturing and logistics use data to eliminate "trial-and-error," simulating business decisions in digital twins before implementing them to ensure guaranteed ROI.

    • Example: Amazon doesn't just wait for you to buy something; it uses data to stay three steps ahead of the "Purchase" button through its use of Anticipatory Shipping and its AI shopping assistant, Rufus.
