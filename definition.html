What is data war?

A data war is a strategic competition where entities strive to control and leverage information to gain a decisive advantage. It involves using massive datasets and AI to optimize decision-making, influence behaviors, and outperform rival organizations. The objective is to achieve information dominance, ensuring superior market positioning, national security, or global digital influence.

The Three Main Fronts:

  • Commercial (The Market Race):
    Companies compete to gather the most consumer data. The winner can predict trends, target ads with surgical precision, and build the most powerful AI tools. If a competitor has better data, they have a better product.
  • National Security:
    Governments struggle for "data sovereignty." This involves protecting their citizens' information from foreign influence while trying to gain intelligence on adversaries to tip the balance of power.
  • Ethical (Privacy vs. Access):
    The tension between an individuals right to keep their life private and an organization’s desire to turn that life into a data point for profit or security.

    • Example: The U.S. government mandated the divestment of TikTok’s American operations (finalized in early 2026) to prevent foreign access to the data of 150 million citizens.
